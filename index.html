Projects

Temple College of Science Á Technology Transfer Course Evaluation Tool

Currently, in my senior capstone class at Temple University I am working with the CIS (College of Science and Technology) advising department with a group of 5 other students to help develop a tool for the department to use that will help them keep track of, and manage courses that new Temple students are attempting to tranfer from other universities. We are currently still on the planning stages of this tool as part of the capstone 1 course curriculum but will hopefully be able to start development in the second part of the course. It has been a great learning experience so far being able to meet with our clients (a number of Temple advisors) and working through the planning process.

Mobile Application Hackathon Contest

In September of 2020 I signed up for a mobile application hackathon event through a company called V.One. I designed a financial tracking mobile app on my own to be submitted to the competition. Though I unfortunately didn't win, I had a great time trying out a new developing tool and got a great resonse of votes for my app in the process. I loved working on a project outside of school and feeling proud of the product I created, knowing that it was my product I created on my own. I look forward to trying out more opportunities like this in the future.

Video Game Review Website Project

During one of my Temple courses, Client-Side Scripting for the Web, myself and my partner developed a fully functioning video game review website that utilized much of the material we learned in the class such as HTML, CSS, Javascript, C#, and database management. We did very well on the assignment and I walked away with a project that I felt very proud of and could take with me as an excellent example to reference for my remaining semesters at school and for any independent projects like it that I would like to persue.

Blender

Fun fact about me: Blender is one of my favorite hobbies where I am learning to 3D render photorealistic images.

This chess board is a remake of one of my earliest renders. About two years after making the original I decided to make it look better since my skills in 3D modeling had improved. Though not my best work ever, this chess board was a lot of fun to make and design and stands as one of my favorite projects.

The kitchen depicted in this render was actually designed by my mom, as well as the rest of the house that isn't visible in the picture. Her dream of designing her own house gave me the idea to show her, as best I could, what it could look like in real life. The focus of this project was attention to detail and though it isn't yet finished, I am very proud of what I've done.

This render is one that I am very proud of because of the time that I put into it. This is my first college dorm room modeled almost to scale with items from both myself and my roomate in it. I ran into a lot of problems with this render because this was meant to test my skills and to force myself to grow as a 3D artist. I learned a lot and came out with possibly my favorite project.

This is one of my more current finished projects and the result of an attempted style change. I typically work on photorealism but for this render I tried an abstract piece. This project went surprisingly smoothly and took very little time. Despite that, I am very proud of my first attempt at trying something new and because of it will most likely continue with this type of style.
